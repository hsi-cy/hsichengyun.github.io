Programming

Python programming language is weirdly attractive to me. Having tried and given it up for two consecutive years, in 2019, at my third attempt, my journey in programming finally took off. I started with basic automation, including web crawling and file management. I used those skills to help my manager in Ecolab to produce product checklist with less effort.

Then I started to explore data science, where I found myself interested in data manipulation and visualisation. To polish my skills in these areas, I have done several mini-projects, including a university assignment for business analytics, and visualised comparisons of COVID-19 cumulative cases in different countries in different period of times.

Apart from data-related projects, I am always keen to automate my repetitive work as much as possible. For instance, I made a caption generator to produce standardised contents for my wildlife account on Instagram.

Along the journey, I have picked up skills like SQL, HTML, CSS, and JavaScript as well. For more information about my projects, please visit my Github repositories.

Photography

Wildlife photography is my hobby. I manage an Instagram account, focusing on urban wildlife to share my belief:

"Nature is all around us. So open your mind, be curious, and care more."

As a boy growing up in a city, I was not close to wildlife whatsoever. Even when I got my first camera in 2013, pictures of city views and landscapes occupied the majority of my memory card. Only a few years later, I accidentally took a good picture of a woodpecker in Russia and realised how fun it is to photograph wildlife!

The most amazing encounter with wild animals I had was in November 2019 seeing a common buzzard landing on a tree right in front of me within 10 metres. It was a breath-taking moment. My eyes were focused on the bird of prey but the image I saw, as a whole, every small bird and animal in the frame was running away. A few months later, hcy_wildlife_photography was online and now it has more than five hundred followers.
